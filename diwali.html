Hinduism: Diwali

Diwali is a festival that isn't only celebrated
by Hindus, it's also celebrated in Skihism, Jainism
Newer Bhuddism of Nepal. It is the festival of lights
and diyas are made of clay and put wax inside it to
light it up. They are used during rituals, ceremonies
and praying time. Although why do we celebrate Diwali?
There is a story in India of Rama and his wife (Sita) being
happy. Then Ravana saw Sita and thought of her being beautiful and
wanted to marry her. Sita refused as in Hindu culture we don't do more than
1 marriage. Ravana didn't like this so he kidnapped Sita and took her to whats now Sri Lanka.
Ravana's transportation of getting there was his invention of an airplane called a Pushpak Viman.
Rama as you can imagine hated this and decided to make a man made bridge all the way to Sri Lanka.
The bridge was made over the sea and has remained as one of humanities greatest feat.
He then ended up getting Sita back by fighting and killing Ravana. When Rama and Sita came back to India, everyone
lit up Diyas to celebrate the victory of good over evil. That's why we celebrate Diwali
In Diwali, Hindus wear new clothes and light fireworks
and worship the goddess of prosperity and wealth, Goddess Lakshmi.
The word Diwali derives from Sanskrit of Depavali meaning row of lights.
